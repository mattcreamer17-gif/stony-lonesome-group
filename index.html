01 The useful frame

Most of what looks like AI is not AI.

In most dual-use companies, about sixty percent of what looks like an AI problem is traditional code and database work. About thirty percent is rule-based logic that belongs in a deterministic layer. Only about ten percent is a genuine large-model problem. The firms that mistake the sixty for the ten waste capital and timeline. The firms that get the split right ship faster, pass federal scrutiny more cleanly, and build customer retention that reads as moat to a Series B syndicate.

60%
Traditional Code & Database
Postgres, indexes, queues, scheduled jobs. The unglamorous layer that ships on time and holds up in procurement review.
30%
Rule-Based Logic
Deterministic policy, thresholds, and routing. Inspectable, testable, defensible under a CFIUS or ATO read.
10%
Genuine Large-Model Work
The ten percent where LLMs actually earn their cost. Context design, retrieval, and evaluation carry most of the weight.
02 The engagement
